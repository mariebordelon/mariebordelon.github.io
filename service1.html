STEM

Congress of Future Science and Technology Leaders

As a rising junior, I attended the Congress of Future Science and Technology Leaders. This event consisted of a collection of STEM-related seminars from college professors and Nobel Laureates. This experience introduced me to a multitude of careers and areas of study available in STEM. While attending the congress, I was also inducted to the society of torch and laurel —an honors program for high-achieving high school students who are passionate about STEM.

Girls Who Code

As a rising senior, I took part in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersions program. I was looking to get out of my comfort zone and experience something new on a subject I hardly knew at all. Programming was a struggle from beginning to end, as mistakes were always a guarantee. I used to be afraid of failure, but over time coding taught me how to be resilient. I learned to laugh at myself and to persevere despite my errors. In addition to the jumble of my newly acquired coding skills, I became aware of the struggle for diversity in the technology field. The gender gap in technology has inspired me to become a more active member of my community by volunteering in elementary robotics clubs and after school programs.

Safety Pin

During my time at Girls who code, I put my skills to work and created a website called Safety Pin. My teammates and I created this website intending to provide an accessible and friendly place in which immigrants can attain valuable resources and build deep connections across the country.
